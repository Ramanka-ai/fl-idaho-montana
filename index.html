Afternoon — Big Springs + Quake Lake en route to Ennis
1:30 PM
Big Springs — feed the giant trout
~20 min off US-20 · ~45 min stop · National Natural Landmark
›

Headwaters of the Henry's Fork. 120 million gallons/day of crystal-clear 52°F water gushing from the ground. National Natural Landmark.

The bridge: Crosses the spring outlet. Coin-operated fish-feeding dispensers. Giant rainbow + brook trout (protected from fishing here, so they get massive) congregate at the foot of the bridge waiting to be fed. Pat will love this.

Bonus on-site: Johnny Sack Cabin (1930s German immigrant, hand-built with a miniature water wheelhouse), 1/2-mile ADA-accessible interpretive trail, often moose sightings.

Directions: From Macks Inn (just past Last Chance), turn east on Big Springs Loop Rd / FS 059. ~4.5 miles paved to the bridge. Free, no entrance fee.

📍 Maps 📍 Big Springs → Quake Lake
3:00 PM
Quake Lake visitor center
US-287 between W. Yellowstone & Ennis · ~30 min stop
›

The story: August 17, 1959 — 7.5-magnitude earthquake at 11:37 PM triggered an 80-million-ton landslide that buried Rock Creek Campground, killed 28 people, and dammed the Madison River to create this 6-mile-long lake. Trees still stick out of the water, drowned where they stood.

Visitor center has the timeline, survivor stories, geology displays. Walk out for the lake view. Sobering, fascinating, fast — 30 min total.

Note: West Yellowstone temptation — drive through, don't stop. Tourist-trap saltwater taffy. Push west on US-287.

Axolotl Lakes — drive in to the lakesConditional
3+ mi dirt road · ~45 min at the lakes
›
⚠️ THREE-CONDITION GO/NO-GO — verify all three before turning off:

1. 4WD vehicle confirmed? Standard SUV rentals are 2WD. Must be specifically reserved as 4WD.
2. BLM Dillon road conditions checked Friday afternoon? Call (406) 683-8000 before leaving Madison Valley Vista. Ask: "Is Axolotl Lake Road #42 dry and passable?" If they say wet/muddy or "4WD only" — abort.
3. No-shame turnaround rule. If 200 yards in the road looks worse than expected — turn around. Don't push through.

If any condition fails: skip Axolotl, continue 25 min to Virginia City, get an extra hour there.

The story: Wilderness Study Area, 40-acre BLM rec area at ~6,900 ft. Multiple small lakes, lush meadows, Madison Range views. Home to the rare axolotl salamander (a non-metamorphosing tiger salamander that keeps juvenile traits its whole life). Plaque on a rock at the lake.

Drive-in: Through the gate with the cattle guard, west end of the parking area. 3.1 miles dirt road (blind corners, steep descent). At intersection turn LEFT (east), 0.7 miles. Lakes are visible from the road. ~45 min on-site (walk the lakeshore, find the plaque, photos).
